Early Life

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, was a distinguished Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He came from a modest Muslim family and pursued his education in physics and aeronautical engineering, graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology and St. Joseph’s College...

Career

Kalam played a pivotal role in India’s missile and nuclear programs. He was instrumental in the development of India’s space and missile technology, leading projects such as the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), which resulted in the successful development of key missiles like AGNI and PRITHVI. This significant contribution earned him the title “Missile Man of India..
